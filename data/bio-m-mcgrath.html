Michael McGrath is Director of the Scottish Catholic Education Service and has served Catholic education in Scotland for 38 years. He taught English and Religious Education in various Catholic schools before PCC Events ACU 2013 Page 8 of 14 becoming Depute Head Teacher of Holyrood Secondary School in Glasgow, the largest Catholic school in Europe. Thereafter, for seven years he served as Head Teacher (Principal) of Our Lady's High School in Cumbernauld, 15 miles north of Glasgow.

In 2003 he was appointed by the Bishops of Scotland as the first Director of the Scottish Catholic Education Service which has established a fine reputation for supporting the work of Scotlandâ€™s 380 Catholic schools. He is Convenor of the Board of Catholic Education at the University of Glasgow where, for many years, he has taught a postgraduate course on Catholic School Leadership. He is a member of the General Teaching Council of Scotland.

Michael first visited Australia in 1998 to attend the ACU Educational Leadership Conference. Since then he has been happy to welcome many Australian colleagues to Scotland.

Dr Gregory Hine

Lecturer
The University of Notre Dame Australia, AUSTRALIA

Session Times

Gregory Hine's teaching career spans 14 years. He has taught in Australia and the United States predominantly in the areas of mathematics, science, and religious education, to middle and high school students. For the past seven years Greg has taught at Seton Catholic College, Samson.

Greg has also held the pastoral position of House Coordinator at Seton for five years. Additionally, Greg has worked as an academic consultant for Academic Associates (Perth) and Pearson Education (Melbourne). He has recently written and published two revision texts for the WACE 3A/3B Mathematics course, and perennially marks TEE papers in Mathematics for the Curriculum Council. He currently is co-authoring a new series of Upper School texts for the Australian Curriculum: Mathematics.

Since 2009, Greg has worked as a sessional lecturer at the University of Notre Dame, Australia. In December 2012, Greg completed his doctoral studies at the University of Notre Dame Australia, Fremantle campus, where he is a full-time staff member in the School of Education. His research investigated student leadership in schoolbased programs, and particular attention was given to exploring how one secondary school developed leadership potential in adolescents. He has published and presented on the topic of adolescent leadership development, and he teaches units on general pedagogy, action research methodology, and mathematics education.

Carmel is a lecturer, researcher and writer in the fields of Spirituality and in Medieval Studies. She holds a doctorate in medieval religious literature and has taught in that area at Macquarie University, the Broken Bay Institute (BBI), the Catholic Institute of Sydney (CIS) and the United Theological College (CSU).

She writes for both academic and general audiences and her diverse publications include the monograph Mysticism and Space (2008); '”The Parable of the Lord and Servant”: Salvific Memory: Gracious Predestination in Augustine and Julian of Norwich' (co-authored with Joseph Lam Cong Quy (2010); 'Cloak and Dagger: Chaucer,Borges and Eco' in A Universal Argentine: Jorge Luis Borges, English Literature and Other Inquisitions (2009); 'Oscar Wilde's The Disciple: A Reflection' (2009); 'Bruce Dawe' in International Dictionary of Literary Biography: Australian Writers, 1950-1975. Vol. 289 (2004). Carmel is currently also working with the Catholic Schools Office of Broken Bay on the pedagogical design of online learning modules in Leading Learning.

My name is Jamie Madigan I have worked in Catholic secondary schools for 19 years and I am currently Deputy Principal of Student Wellbeing at Caroline Chisholm Catholic College in Braybrook, Victoria.

Caroline Chisholm Catholic College currently has an enrolment of approximately 1,500 students, 120 teaching staff and 60 support staff. The College was amalgamated from 3 foundation schools in 1997. The College community currently has 58 nationalities represented in our student cohort and is situated in a low socio-economic area in Melbourne.

The College is comprised of three sub-schools: Junior Boys School (Years 7-9 boys); Junior Girls School (Years 7-9 girls); Senior School (Years 10-12 girls and boys).

I have previously held various leadership roles in the College. These include:

  • H ead of Year 7-9 Junior Boys School
  • H ead of Year 10-12 Senior School
  • L earning and Teaching Coordinator

  • H ealth and Physical Education Coordinator

My current role requires me to be deeply engaged with contemporary educational thought and practice in all areas, with a specific focus on ‘student wellbeing’. Over the years I have immersed myself in all facets of pastoral care, where the development of the whole person is of paramount importance. The building of selfesteem and a connectedness to College life through activities such as retreats, camps, academic competitions, sports and community service are a vital element of overall student wellbeing. Furthermore the development of the whole person within the Catholic school environment based on Gospel values also contributes significantly to student self-efficacy and wellbeing.

I have recently completed my Masters of Educational Leadership at Australian Catholic University. In 2012 I also attended an International Educational Study Tour and visited over 25 high achieving schools throughout Finland England and Scotland.

Leadership and the Development of Family School Partnerships

This presentation draws upon research findings emerging from a study conducted on behalf of the Catholic Education Commission of Victoria on family school partnerships and student learning in low socio-economic communities. The study was conducted over a three year period , 2010-2012, and involved the collection of qualitative and quantitative data from system officials, principals and members of school leadership teams, school staff, parents , students and community members associated with 46 schools in dioceses across Victoria. The study highlighted the vital importance of leadership by system officials, principals, and school based personnell in bringing about reform in family school partnerships. In this presentation we will explore the lessons learned about leadership, both at system and school level

Cultivating a Positive School Culture in a Catholic High School Setting: The Principal's Role

There is a growing body of research about the important role of the school principal in cultivating a positive school culture. School culture influences all happenings in a school. This therefore impacts upon an individual behaviour, expectations and interactions with others. It follows that cultivating and sustaining a positive school culture should be one of the highest priorities for all school principals. However, there is limited research; to gain a detailed insight into strategies principals can implement to improve the culture of the school.

The results from the research highlight that the principal has a pivotal and crucial role in the cultivation of a positive school culture. The strategies used by the principal in this research were consistent with the literature on the role of school principals and school culture. It was found that articulating a shared vision, developing a sense of belonging amongst members of the community, developing collaboration between staff, good communication skills, clearly visible in supporting students’ endeavours and treating people fairly were some of the strategies that cultivate a positive school culture.

The findings of this research can serve as a framework for Catholic school principals to cultivate a positive culture within their own schools.

Mr Michael Stewart, Principal, St Mary's Catholic Primary School

Teacher Wellbeing – Time for Action!

We care for the wellbeing of our students but how much do we care for the wellbeing of our teachers? As the major resource in any educational institution do we really look after them and give them strategies to maintain their vibrancy and creativity? Should we accept that 50 hours of intensive work each week has no effect on the teacher’s health and general wellbeing? Is it solely the responsibility of each teacher to manage their own wellbeing or do the educational institution, unions and Government have a role to play? In my 29 years of teaching, I have received very little in the way of support and strategies to help me look after myself. Sorry, enough is enough and now as a Senior Leader, I am doing something about it. This is the one presentation this year that you do not want to miss: music, humour and practical ideas to take away. A look at the data on teachers’ health should scare most people into action. What are Leadership teams within our educational institutions doing to look after their teachers and what strategies are available? Come and share your ideas as it is time for action on teacher wellbeing.

Mr Ian Vickers, Assistant Principal, Sancta Maria College, AUCKLAND, NEW ZEALAND

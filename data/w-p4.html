Curriculum Challenges and Dilemmas for Catholic School Leadership

Drawing on his extensive Irish and limited Australian experience and on relevant literature, the author identifies key leadership challenges and dilemmas for Catholic schools at the macro, meso and micro levels. These include the identification and realisation of the 'added value' of Catholic schools in the light of that Church's social teaching, with particular reference to the formal curriculum. The related issues are located in the broader context of the prevailing neo-liberal culture of performativity, the competing discourses of management and leadership and current thinking regarding teacher professionalism and school development. Implications for school leadership are considered along with lessons for the future.

Dr Jim Gleeson, Professor of Identity and Curriculum, Australian Catholic University
